Publications

  • Text-based Geolocation of German Tweets

    Published at NLP4CMC 2015

    In this follow-up to my work of 2014, I develop an approach to predict the geolocation of Tweets exclusively by using their words. In contrast to my previous work where we classified Tweets into multiple geographical regions, I now predict the exact location from where a Tweet was sent. Even though regression is often harder to solve than classification, my new approach performs 50% better regarding accuracy and can locate Tweets with a median distance of 7km.

    Download PDF

    • State Splitting for Interpreted Regular Tree Grammars

  • Bachelor Thesis

    In my bachelor thesis, I discuss a state splitting algorithm for interpreted regular tree grammars. While most approaches perform on PCFGs and require researchers to select states by hand, I concentrate on an entirely automatic approach introduced by Petrov et al. (2006). I apply the algorithm to IRTGs so that state splitting can be performed for various algebras and grammar formalisms like TAGs, LCFRS, and even semantic graphs.

    Download PDF

    • Mapping German Tweets to Geographic Regions

  • Published at KONVENS 2014

    The paper was the result of a project I worked on for a class about the computational linguistical analysis of Twitter data in the summer of 2013. Together with two fellow students, we found a way to detect the geographical origins of a tweet exclusively by analyzing the words it contains. One of the main results of our research was that most German words have no regional bias and can therefore not be used for our approach. Nevertheless, when using more regional salient tokens, we could achieve an accuracy of more than 50% at a random baseline of 14%.
